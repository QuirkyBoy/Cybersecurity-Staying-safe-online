Safety in numbers

When creating a password, the general gist is the more characters the better, but we begin to run into problems eventually, such as trying to remember it and not wanting to spend lots of time typing when you just want to browse your favorite website. Here are some things you can do to counter this:

  1. The most imprtant is to have a variety of different types of characters, such as letters, numbers, uppercase or lowercase or even things like !, # or @
  2. Another thing is to not follow regular patterns. Most people will do a word, normally their pet or something they're interested in with an uppercase for the first letter, then a number, often their year of birth. To make your password harder to guess, don't follow this format and make it more random.
  3. Make sure your password isn't easily traceable, such as your favourite pet or animal, or your best friend's name which can often be easily found on public social media accounts. Your birthday can also be easily found on these sites, so avoid using them.

If you follow this advice, you should be able to create a strong password that is hard to guess, but easy to remember. If you want to increase security further, change your password around once a month, following the rules above.
