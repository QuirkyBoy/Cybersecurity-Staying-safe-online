Cookies

Cookies are small files that are stored on your computer. They are used to remember your preferences and settings. Cookies are usually stored on your computer for a short period of time. They can be deleted by clicking the button on the bottom of the page. HTTP cookies (also called web cookies, Internet cookies, browser cookies, or simply cookies) are small blocks of data created by a web server while a user is browsing a website and placed on the user's computer or other device by the user's web browser. Cookies are placed on the device used to access a website, and more than one cookie may be placed on a user's device during a session. (Source)

However, you must be careful when accepting cookies. Some websites or apps will have cookies that give permission to the company to collect and store your data for their own purposes. Some companies who are known to do this are Facebook, who have permission to release and use videos and images that you post wherever they want when you accept cookies, and Twitter. These types of cookies are called tracking cookies. Tracking cookies keep track of what you do online and collect information about you. Most of the time, marketing, advertising, and search engine companies use them to show you ads based on what you do on the internet (Source).
